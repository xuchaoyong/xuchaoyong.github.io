Reading Note 1 of The power of habit

The Habit Loop

Over time, this loop-cue cue: a signal to a performer to begin a specific speech or action, routine, reward; cue, routine, reward - becomes more and more automatic.

  • If you want to build a habit, you’d better take advantage of the loop procedure. Firstly, you need a goal to motivate
    yourself; Secondly, you should practice the behavior repeatedly again and again to become a routine; Thirdly, reward
    will enhance the memory of your brain and accelerate the process, which I often ignored.

Habit and Brain

Habits emerge because the brain is constantly looking for ways to save effort. The brain will try to make almost any
routine into a habit, because habits allow our minds to ramp down more often.

Our brains automatically make routine into habits which can not be controlled by ourselves. This explains why it’s
so hard to create exercise habits, for instance, or change what we eat. Once we develop a routine of sitting on the
couch, rather than running, or snacking whenever we pass a doughnut box, these patterns always remain inside our
heads.

By the same rule, though, if we learn to create new neurological routines that overpower those behaviors - if we take
control of the habit loop - we can force those bad tendencies into the backgroud.

  • The habit is builded unconsciously by your brain automatically to reduce the its load and routine offer the conditions
    to your brain, reward help the procedure. Therefore, if you want to prevent a bad behavior from being a habit, you
    should avoid the routine and give punishment. On the other hand, for example, you want to create exercise habits, you should provide the routine and reward to your brain. Then it will be easier for you to persist on the behavior without efforts.
